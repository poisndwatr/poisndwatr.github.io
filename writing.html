About:

Writing is a way to capture both the world and the people living in it. I love being able to create worlds and stories but mostly being able to explore emotions and concepts. I think different mediums of writing are each powerful in their own rights. I enjoy the way that in poetry I can explore emotions and events in odd way focusing less on a world and a story and more on a concept. For me writing poetry feels like making a collage, I am carefully selecting what goes where to tell a story and paint a picture. In that metaphor writing fiction would be photography or painting. It is still art, it is still precisely crafted but it is bigger. Blending the two and experimenting out of the bounds they typically set is similar.

Poetry:

Teeth Shut

Published in 125.1 edition of the Coraddi magazine

Why
Runs from my mouth
Like it has become sentient
And afraid of me eating it,
Grinding it between my teeth,
Hiding it under my tongue,
And swallowing it.
Because I know better
Than to let it escape so easily.
And it dances in circles around us,
Your eyes bearing into me
Like I am a criminal,
The villain in this story,
Not for holding the word captive,
But for letting it even exist.
And I try to catch it like a fly
Clapping my hands around wildly
Before anyone can see what I have done,
Because if I'm not fast enough,
Security will be increased
And I will be locked up along with it.
Sentenced to life
In my mind.

art

Sitting on a cold white display,
a light shines down on me, blinding,
I don’t see the thought that creeps
alongside the strangers approaching
with a point, a smile, all the while
whispering, “I love you,” to me, begging
to scratch through the glass they see caged
around me, they dance, offering their hands
and knees, just hoping to be able to reach
my heat-hardened skin, but all I care
to see is the grease their fingertips leave
on my clear casing, their smeared faces
look up at me, unsettling, wondering,

and I wonder what it’s like, to be made
to be loved, my skin was once chipped
away at slowly, meticulously, rough hands
ground against me, and as I suffocated on the pain-
ted smile, “sit pretty,” I was stuffed
into my glass home to finally prove that I am
worthy, to be deemed as something more
than a lump of clay some woman once threw
together, it does not matter, as some
woman once whispered, “you will never be
Loved,”

The Universe Forgiven Series

The Great Ruins

Asseculae are parasites, they must drain humans of their energy, generated by love, to survive. Cas is the first Asseculae to appear in centuries. With no choice but to be a monster, because if they weren't it wouldn't just mean their own death, but potentially everyones, Cas does what they must. But what happens when the way things are always done shifts? Does a carefully crafted reason to continue crumble? Do foundations fall?

Short Story Exerpt

Just Another Day

"Claire’s fingers brushed over the paper on the floor only pushing it farther, unable to grip it. The movement of the bus jostled her and she huffed, pushing down harder and the paper was finally caught but crinkled. She sat up in the ripped bus seat, swaying from the change of position moreso than the actual movement of the bus. Nausea rolled through the tips of her fingers that traced the now crinkled paper. She should’ve just put the paper in her bag, shouldn’t have been in such a hurry to get on the bus as if it would somehow get her home quicker."
